# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

Students and everyday planners who need to capture, organize, and revisit daily tasks and notes quickly. They value low-friction navigation, fast board/page creation, and lightweight collaboration similar to Notion.

## Product Purpose

This product helps users plan daily work and ideas across multiple formats (boards, markdown pages, lists) without losing momentum. It exists to make task organization and collaboration feel immediate, clear, and dependable, from quick personal planning to shared workflows.

## Brand Personality

Premium, playful, focused. The tone should feel Apple-like in restraint and whitespace, Notion-like in task clarity and data orientation, with subtle fun/funky moments through microinteractions rather than visual noise.

## Anti-references

- Generic AI-template aesthetics (predictable palettes, repetitive card scaffolds, overused visual tropes)
- Overly loud, cluttered UIs that reduce scanability and task speed
- Decorative style choices that weaken information hierarchy

## Design Principles

1. Prioritize task velocity over ornament: creation, navigation, and editing paths should stay short and obvious.
2. Let whitespace do structural work: use calm spacing and hierarchy to communicate importance before color or effects.
3. Keep interaction intelligence subtle: microinteractions should confirm state and intent, never distract from work.
4. Maintain familiar power-user patterns: collaboration and data views should feel instantly learnable for Notion-adjacent users.
5. Avoid AI-generic outputs: each surface should have specific purpose-led decisions, not category-default styling.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline across core surfaces, with strong contrast for readable body text and controls. Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` by reducing or removing non-essential animation. Maintain keyboard-first operability for primary workflows (navigation, creation, editing, sharing) with clear visible focus states.
